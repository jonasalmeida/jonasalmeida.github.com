Synopsis - Computational Biology and Data Sciences for Biomedical Systems

In January 2019, after 5 years as Professor and Chief Technology Officer at the Biomedical Informatics Department of Stony Brook University (State University of NY, Long Island), I took a new position as senior investigator and Chief Data Scientist at the Division of Cancer Epidemiology and Genetics (NIH/NCI/DCEG). That, in turn, followed 4 years as the inaugural director of a new Division in Informatics in the Dept of Pathology of the Univ Alabama at Birmingham (UAB), 5 years as Professor of Bioinformatics in the Division of Applied Mathematics of the University of Texas MDAnderson Cancer Center (2006-2010), and 5 years at the Dept of Biostatistics and Bioinformatics at the Medical University of South Carolina (2001-2005). In a nutshell, I’ve always been chasing data on Biomedical Systems and followed them wherever they took me. At DCEG, NCI’s cancer prevention intramural program, I lead the Data Science and Engineering group.

My current research interests are at the intersection of distributed Cloud and Web Computing where Artificial Intelligence finds a natural computational ecosystem, and Precision Prevention finds a scalable platform. For more information see my NCI page.
